Dear Lucy,

The end of a school year is a strange time. Every year so far, I've been one of the last people to head home. I don't remember whether that was a conscious decision I made in past years. This time, at least, I had an exam on Friday, so I chose to move out Saturday. I suppose I could've left on Tuesday - all my exams after Monday are online - but the possibility never really occurred to me. In any case, it feels like a personal tradition now.

It's weird. In our freshman and sophomore years, the last day or two always had a unique atmosphere. There was just a stark contrast between the liveliness of the floor and campus on the second-to-last Friday and that of the final one. This year, that sense is more muted. I guess it's just because there wasn't that much happening on the floor when people were still around.

This semester has been a real mixed bag. On the one hand, the "school" side of school is going great. I've learned a lot of really cool stuff this semester, especially in my out-of-major classes, and there's a decent chance I'll escape with a 4.0 for the term. But it has felt like a bit of a backslide socially. We somehow made it a full semester without getting more than four of [you, me, Nick, Emily, Hayden, Richard] together in one place. Heck, we only rarely managed to get more than three, despite that set of six people including two sets of room/housemates. As far as any sort of collective identity or narrative is concerned, it's like we skipped forwards a year. I'm interested to see what the social scene looks like when we all return.

That's not to say that I'm particularly sad or regretful. I still made new friends this year, and I'd like to think I've maintained all my friendships that really matter. I certainly feel like I've gained some perspective on life. It's just weird to look back on it all now that it's mostly over

I hope you've been as fortunate as I have in these regards. Now that you're a little more removed from things, how do you feel about your semester and year as a whole? Got any bold plans for next year? Any new people to bring to the *excellent* parties Nick, Richard and I will be hosting? Senior year is going to be an exciting time, I expect.

All my best, Edward.
